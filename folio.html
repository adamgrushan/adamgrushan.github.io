Folio Investing
Legal Intern
Abandoned Account Management
Summer 2016

Folio Investing is a self-clearing brokerage dealer based in McLean, Virginia. I interned there the summer of 2016 and worked in the legal and compliance departments on a specific liability the company was facing: a large and unidentified amount of abandoned accounts were held at Folio. State laws required the unwitting owners be made aware of the accounts, and if no contact could be made the accounts were subject to be transferred to the state. If Folio did not comply with the laws at all, it could even face harsh penalties in the case of an audit.

I approached the problem by acknowledging the accounts had to first be identified, so I researched unclaimed property law in all fifty states to determine what constituted an abandoned account. Many different legal permutations had to be considered because the yearly requirements of no transactions or no contact, to be considered abandoned, were different for many states. I put the understanding of those laws into practice as I helped develop an SQL script specific to state laws that parsed through Folio’s entire set of account data and transaction history according to each account owner’s state of domicile and the specific unclaimed property requirements in each state.

At the same time, I knew the law stated that simply identifying the accounts was not sufficient to avoid liability. Once identified, Folio would have to attempt to contact owners and let them know they held an account at Folio. Therefore, I also worked to develop and implement customer service procedures for contacting owners, and in the case of no contact being made, the eventual escheatment of accounts to the state.

The SQL script we ran ultimately flagged over $1 million in abandoned account values, cumulatively in ten states. I finished the internship writing a detailed memo to the legal and compliance teams that specified the next steps to become fully compliant with the law. By the end of my time at Folio, I was more aware of the many challenges financial firms face in complying with the vast amount of financial regulation that exists today.
