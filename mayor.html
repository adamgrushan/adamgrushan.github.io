Los Angeles Mayor’s Office of Public Safety
Legislative Research Intern
Summer 2017

As a summer intern in the Public Safety Office of Los Angeles Mayor Eric Garcetti, I was tasked with a wide range of legislative research projects related to public safety. Most of my time was spent writing extensive research memos on public safety and homeland security policy issues that ranged from local human trafficking efforts (I wrote an abridged version Human Trafficking Efforts and Los Angeles for Medium publication), to legislative effects on public safety (I wrote two Medium articles on related social science topics: On the Importance of Footnotes and On the Importance of Comparing Apples to Apples), to the intersection of federal and state drone policy. These memos all served to brief the Mayor’s Director of Policy and Programs for important meetings and potential policy decisions.

Additionally, I submitted research assessments of LAX and the Los Angeles aerospace manufacturing cluster for consideration as Department of Homeland Security Critical Infrastructure Assets to secure grant funding for the City, and proofread and reviewed both a domestic violence service provider report and a hazard mitigation proposal released through the Mayor’s Office to assure writing clarity and analytical precision.

On the fun side of things, I toured Los Angeles International Airport and its many security measures (including its SWAT team vehicles) and volunteered at the Mayor's 2017 inauguration. Working at City Hall was invigorating and I learned about the many connections the Public Safety Office must maintain to ensure Angelenos’ safety.
