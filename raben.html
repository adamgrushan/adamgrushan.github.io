The Raben Group
Public Policy and Strategic Communications Intern
Public Affairs Consulting
September 2017 – December 2017

After working for the Mayor of Los Angeles the past summer, I wanted to still work on public issues, but experience the private sector side of things. Thus, I pursued the opportunity to work at the Raben Group, a progressive public affairs firm in Los Angeles, and help consult many nonprofit, governmental, and corporate clients on a range of causes.

Most notably, in the wake of the President's decision to end Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals (DACA), I assisted a top media client who requested information regarding the positions of democratic representatives from the House of Representatives on three recently introduced immigration bills: the Development, Relief, and Education for Alien Minors (DREAM) Act, the Recognizing America’s Children (RAC) Act, and the Bar Removal of Individuals who Dream and Grow our Economy Act (BRIDGE) Act.

To tackle this problem, I started by pulling data from the Quorum web application which tracks House members’ sponsorship statuses on legislation. I then exported that data to Google Sheets, where I wrote a series of functions to automatically parse through the data and used formatting tools to present the sponsorship statuses and statements uniformly and plainly. Ultimately, I built and packaged the deliverable sent to the client that presented House Democrats’ formal sponsorship statuses and statements automatically and in a clear manner.

I also leveraged my writing and news media search skills to compose weekly media roundups for various nonprofits interested in the latest criminal justice reform news around the country, and drafted social media posts for eventual publication.

I now recognize the important value public affairs consultants offer to non-profit and corporate teams that seek to effect change on policy issues but do not have the technical knowhow or government connections to see it through.
